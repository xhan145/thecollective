Collective Brand Package
 Small steps. Real progress.
A warm, calm identity system for a progress-and-contribution platform where people discover meaningful directions, practice real growth, submit proof,
receive feedback, build trust, and eventually help others.
Core loop: Discover -> Practice -> Prove -> Get Feedback -> Build Trust -> Contribute
Launch wedge: Confidence, communication, and momentum.

--- page 2 ---
1. Brand Foundation
Product concept
Collective is not a normal social media app, course app, or coaching marketplace. It is a place to practice useful
growth, submit proof, receive feedback, and earn trust through contribution.
Brand promise
Progress you can build on.
Personality
Warm, calm, useful, trustworthy, beginner-safe, lightly premium, never performative.
Principles
Real progress over appearance. Practice over passive content. Usefulness over attention. Contribution over clout.
Earned trust, not paid trust. AI support, not AI authority.

--- page 3 ---
2. Logo System
The mark represents many small practices, proofs, feedback notes, and contributions forming a shared open center. It should feel communal and
active, not like a generic sun, star, flower, or badge.
Primary mark
Horizontal lockup
App icon direction
Usage: Use the large mark on Home and onboarding. Use the mini mark in sheets, empty states, success states, and secondary screens. Keep it quiet
and persistent without making the app feel like an advertisement.
Do not: Stretch, rotate, recolor randomly, put on loud backgrounds, or use it as a clout badge.

4. Typography and UI Shape
Use the native system font stack. Do not package proprietary fonts. On Android, default Compose/Roboto is acceptable. On iOS, SF Pro is acceptable.
On web, use Inter if available, then system sans.
Screen background
#FFF8EE warm cream
Cards
#FFFDF8, 18dp radius, subtle elevation, no heavy borders
Sheets
28dp top radius, small centered mini mark, calm title, direct CTA
Buttons
Gold fill for primary actions. Tonal cream/gold for secondary actions.
Motion
Subtle. Use progress and completion animations. Avoid confetti and pressure.
